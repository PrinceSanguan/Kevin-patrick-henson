Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup made with pork, vegetables, and tamarind. This hearty and comforting dish is perfect for rainy days and is known for its signature sour and savory taste that Filipinos love.

Prep Time

20 minutes

Cook Time

1 hour

Servings

6-8 people

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 2 pieces tamarind soup base (or fresh tamarind)
  • 2 pieces medium-sized tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 pieces radish, sliced
  • 2 pieces eggplant, sliced
  • 200g string beans, cut into 2-inch length
  • 1 bundle water spinach (kangkong)
  • 4 pieces green chili
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 8-10 cups water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the pork belly. Simmer for about 30 minutes or until meat is tender.
  2. Add the onions and tomatoes. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the tamarind soup base and stir until well combined.
  4. Add the radish and cook for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Add the eggplant and string beans. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  7. Add the water spinach and green chili. Cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Turn off heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!

Tips

  • Use pork with some fat for better flavor
  • Don't overcook the vegetables to maintain their crunch
  • Adjust the sourness by adding more or less tamarind
  • You can add other vegetables like okra or taro
